# Lesson 2 delivered: Tea, food & Bangladeshi hospitality

First session using the teach skill. No prior learning records existed — this session
established a baseline and delivered the second lesson in the curriculum.

## What was established as prior knowledge

Lesson 1 existed but no record of completion. The session opened with retrieval practice
on Lesson 1 material (arrival greetings, "kemon achen" response, Amma address, "ashun")
to test retention before building on it.

## What was taught

The hospitality scenario: the first 30 minutes after arriving at the in-laws' home.
Core phrases: "cha khorben?", "jee shukriya", "khub shundor hoyeche", "bhalo laglo",
"aro neen", "ektu nebo", "ami bhoraa", "khub mishti", "moja holo!"
Listening patterns: the full hospitality loop as dialogue exchanges.
Bonus small talk: kaaj kaamon cholche?, bacchara kaamon ache?, shoreer kaamon?

## Dhaka-specific points highlighted

- "Khorben" vs standard "khaben" — Dhaka hospitality verb
- "Moja" as a warm, distinctively Dhaka expression of enjoyment
- "Aro neen" as an act of love, not a literal command
- Asking about elders' health (shoreer kaamon?) as a sign of deep respect

## What to revisit next session

- Confirm retrieval of Lesson 1 phrases held (check via warm-up)
- The distinction between "khub shundor hoyeche" (process compliment) vs "khub mishti" (taste)
- Transition into small talk: the learner now has openers but no follow-through phrases

## What to teach next

Natural next scenario: **holding a short conversation** — responding when they ask about
*you* (your work, your health, your family). The learner can now greet and survive the
meal; next they need to handle being asked questions and giving simple answers about
their own life. Lesson 3 candidate: "They're asking about you — how to answer simply."
