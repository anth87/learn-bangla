# MISSION.md Format

`MISSION.md` lives at the workspace root. It captures the _reason_ the user is learning this topic — not just what they want to learn, but why, and what success actually looks like in the real world.

## Template

```md
# Mission: {Topic}

## Why
{1-3 sentences on why this matters to the learner. Personal, concrete, honest.}

## Success looks like
- {A specific, observable thing the learner can do when they've succeeded}
- {Another observable outcome}
- {Another observable outcome}

## Constraints
- {Time available per session}
- {Budget, tools, prior commitments}
- {Prior knowledge level}

## Out of scope
- {Adjacent topics that are explicitly not being pursued right now}
- {Scope boundaries that prevent rabbit-holing}
```

## Rules

- **One mission per workspace.** If the learner has two goals, create two workspaces.
- **Concrete over abstract.** "Hold a 5-minute conversation with my mother-in-law" beats "become conversational."
- **Push back on vagueness.** If the learner writes "understand Bangla better," ask: understand it well enough to do what, specifically?
- **Revise when reality shifts.** The mission is not a contract — it should be updated when the learner's situation changes.
- **Keep it short.** A long, hedged mission is a sign of unclear thinking. If it won't fit on a phone screen, trim it.

## When to revisit

- At the start of a new learning block (new term, new quarter)
- When the learner expresses frustration or disengagement
- When the learner hits a milestone and needs a new challenge
- When life circumstances change (job, move, relationship)
