# GLOSSARY.md Format

`GLOSSARY.md` lives at the workspace root. It is a living vocabulary reference — updated after each lesson as new terms are introduced.

## Template

```md
# Glossary: {Topic}

> Phonetic spellings only. {Any notes on the romanisation system used.}

## {Section Name}

**term (phonetic)**: definition. *Context or usage note.*
  - _Also heard as_: variant pronunciations
  - _Avoid_: a common mistake or false friend to steer clear of

**another term (phonetic)**: definition.
```

## Rules

- **Phonetic first.** In this workspace: Bengali script is never used. All entries are phonetic romanisation only.
- **Group by meaning, not alphabet.** Thematic groupings (Greetings, Family, Food, etc.) are more useful than alphabetical lists for language learning.
- **Add on introduction.** Add a term to the glossary the session it is first taught, not retroactively.
- **Mark difficulty.** If a term caused confusion in a session, note it: *[review needed]*.
- **Include variants.** Dhaka dialect often differs from textbook Bangla — note both where relevant.
- **Keep definitions brief.** One line. Context notes can be a second line. Not a dictionary entry.
- **_Avoid_ aliases.** Where a learner is likely to use a wrong form (e.g. a Kolkata variant instead of Dhaka), flag it explicitly.

## Sections to consider for Bangla

- Greetings & Courtesy
- Address & Family Titles
- Everyday Conversation
- Questions & Responses
- Food & Meals
- Feelings & States
- Time & Place
- Numbers & Quantities
